From the sordid space of misinterpreting and mislabeling black men emerges the Thug Myth Project —a passion project aimed at debunking one of many myths about the black man. Enough is enough of portraying our black men as threats to society. More asset than accomplice, more valuable than vandal, more leader than lackey—black men are pillars in the community and it is vital to recognize and honor the many roles they play that are anything but thuggish.

We are swapping stories. We are assembling narratives. We are shouting out to all the men of our villages to stand strong in their skin—a skin so often objectified by a world lost in labels. Using our words not our fists, our tears not our blood, our hearts not our hate—we are dictating that not only do black lives matter, they are necessary. We must take control of our own images so consistently appropriated or distorted into a reason to take us out.

Pointing cameras not guns, the Thug Myth Project will capture and reflect images of the black man in a way that is far too absent from today’s entertainment line up. The lives taken are real. So these lives portrayed are purposed at saving life—at ripping apart that ugly thug myth.

So tell us—what’s your story?
